From Cards to Consoles: Nintendo's Journey

1889-1956: Humble Beginnings

Founded on September 23, 1889, by Fusajiro Yamauchi in Kyoto, Nintendo began as Nintendo Koppai - a small workshop crafting hanafuda (flower cards). These intricate playing cards:

  • Made from durable mulberry bark paper (washi)
  • Hand-painted with traditional Japanese motifs
  • Featured 48 cards divided into 12 floral months

Initially sold to exclusive gambling parlors, the cards gained popularity through:

  • Strategic partnerships with tobacco shops (1907)
  • Introduction of Western-style playing cards (1927)
  • Durable laminated coating innovation (1933)

Despite challenges like WWII paper shortages and the 1929 Great Depression, Nintendo became Japan's premier card manufacturer under President Sekiryo Kaneda (Yamauchi's son-in-law), establishing:

  • First corporate headquarters in Kyoto (1933)
  • Mass production facilities (1947)
  • Distribution partnership with Disney (1959)
